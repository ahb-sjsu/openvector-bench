\begin{abstract}
Summary statistics of embedding geometry --- intrinsic dimension, effective rank,
hubness skew, ball-growth slope --- are routinely treated as properties of a corpus
and are widely believed to trade off against one another. We show that several of
these trade-offs are \emph{representational artifacts}: they appear when a
generative mechanism (or a learned representation) binds distinct geometric layers
to a single control, and they dissolve when the layers are given separate
mechanisms. We propose a four-layer decomposition of embedding geometry --- global
metric/spectral structure, corpus measure $\muC$, query measure $\muQ$, and local
scale structure --- and support it with a pre-registered falsification chain over a
procedural corpus-generator search (seven experiment rounds against a real
$1024$-dimensional text-embedding corpus). Three constructive separations result:
(i) angular hub structure vs.\ global spectrum, an apparently sharp Pareto
frontier dissolved by splitting placement from colouring; (ii) \emph{same hubness,
different anatomy}: a synthetic corpus and the real corpus share the same scalar
hubness reading produced by opposite mechanisms (centrality-driven super-hubs,
max reverse-$k$NN count 369, vs.\ density-driven moderate hubs, max count 78) ---
one-number hubness is non-identifying; (iii) hubness vs.\ ball growth, dissolved
by recognising that the real system's hubness lives substantially in the query
marginal: writing hubness as $H_k(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q},d)$, reverse-neighbour
counts sample the query mass of top-$k$ capture basins,
$N_k(x_j)\approx|\mathcal{Q}|\,\muQ(\capture{x_j})$. We complement the
empirical chain with an \emph{independent-realizability theorem}: the
achievable hub vectors of a fixed corpus form a polytope (the convex hull of
retrievable top-$k$ incidence vectors), and an arbitrarily small
simplex-address augmentation --- which preserves every corpus-to-corpus
neighbour ordering exactly and perturbs the spectrum by $O(\delta)$ ---
expands that polytope to the full hypersimplex $\Delta(n,k)$, so no universal
functional law of the form $G6=f(G2,G3,G7,G8)$ can hold over finite retrieval
experiments. We conclude that apparent geometric trade-offs can be artifacts
of forcing corpus metric, corpus measure, query measure, and local scale
through a shared mechanism, propose a hub \emph{anatomy vector} as the
reporting standard, and derive a registered anatomy falsifier that prevents
optimizers from reproducing a scalar by rebuilding a known pathology. The
falsifier promptly earned its keep: in the final round the optimizer satisfied
all six registered gate bands numerically while the anatomy check convicted it
of rebuilding corpus super-hubs (base-to-base skew $\approx 7$, max
reverse-count $\approx 350$) despite having a genuine query-measure knob
available --- one-number geometry invites Goodharting that only anatomy-level
registration catches. Pricing the anatomy into the fitness and applying one
Jacobian-informed correction then closed the campaign: all six registered
gates within $[0.5,2.0]\times$ \emph{and} anatomy in band
(skew $1.4$--$1.9$, max count $52$--$104$ vs.\ real $1.5$/$78$) on three
fresh seeds --- real's numbers by real's mechanism.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The setting is a benchmark-admission problem. OpenVector Bench requires a
procedural corpus generator whose measured geometry matches real embeddings
across eight registered gates --- intrinsic dimension, ball-growth slope,
effective rank, dims-to-90\%, relative contrast, hubness skew, local-ID
spread, and PCA retention (G1--G8) --- in two measurement batteries:
corpus$\to$corpus and held-out-queries$\to$corpus. The search for such a
generator was run under a falsification discipline: before every gating round
a prediction and a named falsifier were registered; every miss was reported as
a miss; a sealed evaluation set was never opened. This paper is about what
that discipline surfaced.

The mystery arose midway through the campaign. Successive generator families
solved every gate \emph{individually} --- intrinsic dimension, rank, PCA
retention, local-ID spread, hubness each fell to some construction --- yet
each new family hit a hard \emph{pairwise} wall. In round 5, hubness
$\times$ effective rank: twelve independent search shards (480 evaluations)
mapped a clean two-ended Pareto frontier with nothing in the middle. In
rounds 6--7, hubness $\times$ ball growth. Walls of this shape invite a
physics reading: a genuine trade-off, a conservation law of embedding
geometry, a reason the benchmark cannot be passed. The reading is wrong ---
and showing \emph{why} it is wrong turns out to be more useful than passing
the benchmark.

The resolution is that the walls were properties of \emph{mechanism binding},
not of the statistics. When one generative control (a Zipf codebook hierarchy,
a shared immersion, a single query recipe) sets two geometric quantities at
once, those quantities trade off \emph{under that family} --- and the
frontier dissolves the moment the layers are given separate mechanisms. The
contributions are:
\textbf{(C1)} a four-layer decomposition of embedding geometry --- global
metric/spectral structure, corpus measure $\muC$, query measure $\muQ$, and
local scale structure (\S\ref{sec:decomposition});
\textbf{(C2)} the hubness-polytope and independence theorems --- hubness as
$H_k(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q},d)$, a linear image of the query measure, with
universal approximate independence of spectrum, local scale, and query
hubness by explicit construction (\S\ref{sec:theory});
\textbf{(C3)} three constructive empirical separations under pre-registered
protocols (\S\ref{sec:separations});
\textbf{(C4)} the capture-basin account and the non-identifiability of scalar
hubness (\S\ref{sec:hubness});
\textbf{(C5)} the hub anatomy vector $A_{\mathrm{hub}}$, the registered
anatomy falsifier that caught a scalar-gaming optimizer in the wild, and a
generator-specific Jacobian controllability test
(\S\ref{sec:anatomy}, \S\ref{sec:jacobian});
\textbf{(C6)} implications for benchmark design and differential ANN testing
(\S\ref{sec:implications}; companion paper: \emph{Fuzzing the Bond Metric}).

Epistemic status, stated plainly: the decomposition is \emph{strongly
corroborated and materially sharpened} by the falsification chain --- not
universally confirmed. The theorems of \S\ref{sec:theory} establish that no
universal law couples the layers; whether a \emph{particular} family or a
\emph{particular} real embedding distribution couples them is an empirical
question, and \S\ref{sec:limits} reports where coupling is real --- including
at corpus scales beyond the fitting regime, where the campaign's own
registered grid rejected its winning candidate.

\paragraph{Relation to the workload-theory programme.} This paper is the
first output of a registered research programme (\emph{Vector Workload
Normal Forms}, \texttt{spec/NORMAL\_FORMS.md} in the benchmark repository)
whose object of study is the joint workload
$W = (P_X, P_Q, d, k, N)$ and whose target is a compact descriptor
$\Phi(W)$ predicting ANN system behaviour. In the programme's terms, the
four-layer decomposition is the coarse structure of the proposed workload
normal form; the theorems here are its WP1 identifiability limits (the
strongest available ``identical corpus diagnostics, different workload''
construction); and the generator campaign is its WP3 intervention
laboratory. Everything in this paper concerns geometry and measurement ---
whether matched geometry \emph{plus anatomy} transfers to matched
recall--cost behaviour on real ANN indexes is the programme's next
registered gate (WP5), pre-registered at the time of writing.

The four layers are distinct in what they quantify over. The \emph{global
metric/spectral layer} is a property of the ambient inner-product structure
after centering: the covariance spectrum and everything monotone in it. The
\emph{corpus measure} $\muC$ is a distribution over that substrate: where
corpus mass sits, how it clusters, what angular regions it occupies. The
\emph{query measure} $\muQ$ is a second, independent distribution over the
same substrate --- operationally an \emph{illumination pattern} over the
corpus's capture basins (\S\ref{sec:hubness}), and the layer that six rounds
of corpus-side search implicitly ignored. The \emph{local scale structure} is
the within- and across-neighbourhood distance distribution: how the first $k$
neighbour distances of a point relate to each other and how homogeneous that
profile is across query points. None of these is reducible to another: the
theorems of \S\ref{sec:theory} exhibit corpora in which any one is moved
while the others are pinned.

Two general principles complete the frame. \emph{The binding thesis}: a
trade-off observed under one generative family is evidence about that
family's control structure, not about the statistics --- treating a family's
Pareto frontier as a law of geometry is a category error, and
\S\ref{sec:separations} shows the same frontier being erected and dissolved
by moving one mechanism. \emph{Amplification}: in local dimension
$d_{\ell}$, measure-layer observables respond as $s^{-d_{\ell}}$ to
metric-layer perturbations of relative scale $s$, so binding layers is easy
to do by accident and expensive to undo --- tiny metric nudges produce large
measure signatures, which is why an optimizer given a bound mechanism will
find grotesque metric configurations to satisfy a measure target
(\S\ref{sec:anatomy}).

\section{Theory: the hubness polytope and the Bond independence theorem}
\label{sec:theory}

The clean result available here is an \emph{independent-realizability theorem},
not statistical independence. Define a retrieval experiment as the tuple
\[
\mathcal{E} = (X,\ \muC,\ \nuQ,\ d,\ k),
\]
where $X=\{x_1,\dots,x_n\}$ is the corpus, $\muC$ its measure, $\nuQ$ the query
measure, $d$ the metric, and $k$ the retrieval depth. Classical hubness uses
reverse-$k$NN occurrence counts \cite{radovanovic10}; order-$k$ Voronoi cells
partition query space by which $k$ corpus points are retrieved. Throughout,
assume queries in general position (ties $\nuQ$-null).

\subsection{The hubness polytope}

For $|S|=k$ define the order-$k$ cell
$V^{(k)}_S = \{q : \NN_k(q;X) = S\}$ and the normalized query-induced hub mass
$h_i(\nuQ) = \nuQ\{q : i \in \NN_k(q;X)\}$. Every query contributes exactly $k$
selections, so $0 \le h_i \le 1$ and $\sum_i h_i = k$.

\begin{theorem}[Hubness polytope]\label{thm:polytope}
For any fixed corpus and metric, the set of achievable hub vectors is
\[
\mathcal{H}_k(X,d) \;=\; \conv\bigl\{\mathbf{1}_S : V^{(k)}_S \neq \varnothing\bigr\},
\]
where $\mathbf{1}_S$ is the $0/1$ incidence vector of $S$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For each query let $z(q) = \mathbf{1}_{\NN_k(q;X)}$; then
$h(\nuQ) = \int z(q)\,d\nuQ(q)$. Since $z$ takes only the incidence-vector
values of nonempty cells, its expectation lies in their convex hull.
Conversely, given $h = \sum_S p_S \mathbf{1}_S$, choose $q_S \in V^{(k)}_S$ for
each nonempty cell and set $\nuQ = \sum_S p_S\,\delta_{q_S}$.
\end{proof}

Query hubness is thus a \emph{linear image of the query measure} --- not
intrinsically a corpus statistic. Equation~\eqref{eq:capture} is the coordinate
form of this map.

\subsection{The simplex-address lemma: every hub vector becomes reachable}

Theorem~\ref{thm:polytope} is constrained by which cells happen to be nonempty.
An arbitrarily small augmentation removes even that constraint. Let
$Y = \{y_1,\dots,y_n\} \subset S^{d-1}$ be any unit-normalized corpus, and let
$v_1,\dots,v_n$ be the vertices of a regular simplex in $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$
($v_i^\top v_i = 1$, $v_i^\top v_j = -\tfrac{1}{n-1}$ for $i \neq j$,
$\sum_i v_i = 0$). For $0 < \delta < 1$ construct the augmented corpus
\[
x_i = \bigl(\sqrt{1-\delta^2}\; y_i,\ \delta\, v_i\bigr) \in S^{d+n-2}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Corpus anatomy is exactly preserved]\label{lem:preserve}
For $i \neq j$,
$x_i^\top x_j = (1-\delta^2)\, y_i^\top y_j - \tfrac{\delta^2}{n-1}$,
a strictly increasing affine map of the original cosine similarity. Hence every
corpus-to-corpus neighbour ordering, the base-to-base $k$NN graph, base-to-base
reverse-count hubness, and every rank-based local statistic are \emph{exactly}
preserved. For chord distance,
$\|x_i - x_j\|^2 = (1-\delta^2)\|y_i-y_j\|^2 + \tfrac{2n\delta^2}{n-1}$:
the full pairwise-distance matrix undergoes one common monotone
reparameterization.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Every $k$-subset becomes retrievable]\label{lem:address}
For any $S$ with $|S| = k$, let $u_S = \sum_{i \in S} v_i$ and
$q_S = (0,\ u_S/\|u_S\|)$. Then $u_S^\top v_j = \tfrac{n-k}{n-1}$ for
$j \in S$ and $u_S^\top v_j = -\tfrac{k}{n-1}$ for $j \notin S$, so
$q_S^\top x_j$ is strictly larger for every $j \in S$ than for every
$j \notin S$, i.e.\ $\NN_k(q_S; X_\delta) = S$. Every $k$-subset has a
nonempty order-$k$ cell.
\end{lemma}

\begin{corollary}[Universal query-hubness control]\label{cor:hypersimplex}
For the augmented corpus $X_\delta$, the hubness polytope is the full
hypersimplex \cite{ziegler95}:
\[
\mathcal{H}_k(X_\delta) \;=\; \Delta(n,k)
\;=\; \Bigl\{h \in [0,1]^n : \textstyle\sum_i h_i = k\Bigr\}.
\]
Every mathematically feasible query-induced hub vector is realizable exactly
(rational points exactly by finite query sets; arbitrary points to arbitrary
precision), while the complete corpus-to-corpus neighbour graph is unchanged.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Spectral effect of the address block}

The address block's spectral footprint is controlled, though --- one correction
to the naive account --- not exactly diagonal. Writing the covariance of
$X_\delta$ in $(y,v)$ blocks: the diagonal blocks are $(1-\delta^2)\Cov(Y)$ and
$\tfrac{\delta^2}{n-1} I_{n-1}$ (the regular simplex satisfies
$\tfrac1n \sum_i v_i v_i^\top = \tfrac{1}{n-1} I$), so \emph{modulo the cross
term} the padded eigenvalues obey
\[
\lambda_i(X_\delta) \;=\; (1-\delta^2)\,\lambda_i(Y) + \frac{\delta^2}{n-1}
\quad\text{--- an explicit infinitesimal recolouring toward isotropy.}
\]
The cross-covariance
$\delta\sqrt{1-\delta^2}\,M$ with $M = \tfrac1n \sum_i y_i v_i^\top =
\tfrac1n Y^\top V$ is generically nonzero; by Weyl's inequality it perturbs
each eigenvalue by at most $2\delta\sqrt{1-\delta^2}\,\|M\|_2$, and the bound
is \emph{deterministic}: $\|Y\|_2 \le \sqrt{n}$ (unit rows) and
$\|V\|_2 = \sqrt{n/(n-1)}$ (since $V^\top V = \tfrac{n}{n-1}I$), so
\[
\|M\|_2 \;\le\; \frac1n\,\|Y\|_2\,\|V\|_2 \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{n-1}}
\qquad\text{for \emph{every} unit-normalized corpus.}
\]
All spectral effects vanish as $\delta \to 0$, which is what
Theorem~\ref{thm:independence} uses; no genericity assumption is needed
anywhere in the construction. The separation is therefore unusually
strong: query hubness moves by $O(1)$; corpus neighbour anatomy moves by
exactly zero; spectral change is $O(\delta)$.

\subsection{Macro--micro scale separation}

It remains to separate the global spectrum from local ball growth. Choose macro
centres $c_a \in \mathbb{R}^p$ with minimum separation $\Delta > 0$, and for
each a centered local template
$U_a = \{u_{a1},\dots,u_{am_a}\} \subset \mathbb{R}^q$ with weights
$\beta_{a\ell}$, $\sum_\ell \beta_{a\ell} u_{a\ell} = 0$. Construct
$y_{a\ell} = (c_a,\ \epsilon\, u_{a\ell})$ with weight
$\alpha_a \beta_{a\ell}$.

\begin{lemma}[Spectrum and scale occupy orthogonal blocks]\label{lem:macromicro}
The covariance is exactly block diagonal:
\[
\Cov(Y_\epsilon) \;=\; \Cov(C) \,\oplus\, \epsilon^2 \sum_a \alpha_a \Cov(U_a),
\]
(the per-cluster centering kills the cross term identically). The $O(1)$
global spectrum is controlled by the macro centres; the microstructure enters
at $O(\epsilon^2)$. At radius $\epsilon r < \Delta$ no other cluster is
visible, and
$\muC\bigl(B(y_{a\ell}, \epsilon r)\bigr)
= \alpha_a \sum_{j:\|u_{aj}-u_{a\ell}\| \le r} \beta_{aj}$:
the local ball-growth curve is exactly the template's, rescaled in radius ---
precisely the degree of freedom behind ball-growth intrinsic-dimension
estimators \cite{levina04,facco17}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Common-sphere realization --- the bridge to the address block]
\label{lem:bridge}
The macro--micro corpus can be constructed with \emph{all points on a common
sphere}, so that Lemma~\ref{lem:preserve}'s unit-norm hypothesis is met after
one global rescaling:
(i)~\emph{equal-norm macro centres}: for any target covariance $\Sigma$ with
$m$ centres there is a mean-zero ensemble with $\|c_a\| \equiv R$,
$R^2 = \operatorname{tr}\Sigma$ --- take $A$ with $AA^\top = \Sigma$ whose
Gram matrix has constant diagonal (such $A$ exists by Schur--Horn: the
constant diagonal $\operatorname{tr}\Sigma/m$ is majorized by every spectrum),
then $\pm$-double the columns for mean zero;
(ii)~\emph{spherical local templates}: require $\|u_{a\ell}\| \equiv c$, with
$\pm$ pairs on the template sphere preserving the weighted-mean-zero
condition. Registered ball-growth curves remain realizable --- a growth curve
constrains pairwise chordal distances, which points on $S^{q}$ take freely in
sufficient dimension. Then $\|y_{a\ell}\|^2 = R^2 + \epsilon^2 c^2$ is
constant, with \emph{no} compensator coordinate (a compensator would perturb
local distances at $O(\epsilon)$ --- the same order as the structure being
protected --- and is therefore rejected).
\end{lemma}

Without Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge} the composition has a gap: the raw macro--micro
corpus has unequal norms, under which Euclidean address selection can fail
(norm variation is $O(1)$ while the address margin is $O(\delta)$) and
cosine normalization would disturb the exact block structure. One remark
survives even without it: under \emph{cosine} retrieval the address selection
of Lemma~\ref{lem:address} is norm-robust as stated --- every $j \in S$ has
strictly positive inner product with $q_S$ and every $j \notin S$ strictly
negative, so $\NN_k(q_S) = S$ for arbitrary corpus norms; equal norms are
needed for Lemma~\ref{lem:preserve}'s exact anatomy preservation and for
Euclidean retrieval.

\subsection{The Bond independence theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Universal approximate independence of spectrum, local scale,
and query hubness]\label{thm:independence}
Fix (i) a realizable target covariance spectrum $\Lambda$; (ii) finite target
local ball-growth templates; (iii) a retrieval depth $1 \le k < n$; (iv) any
feasible normalized hub vector $h \in \Delta(n,k)$; and (v) any tolerance
$\eta > 0$. Then in sufficiently high finite dimension there exist a corpus
$X$, corpus measure $\muC$, query measure $\nuQ$, and Euclidean or cosine
metric such that: $\|\lambda(\Cov X) - \Lambda\| < \eta$; the registered local
ball-growth profiles differ from their targets by less than $\eta$;
$h_i = \nuQ\{q : i \in \NN_k(q;X)\}$ holds exactly; and $\nuQ$ can be varied
throughout $\Delta(n,k)$ without modifying the corpus or any corpus-only
observable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof construction]
(1)~Equal-norm macro centres realize $\Lambda$
(Lemmas~\ref{lem:macromicro}--\ref{lem:bridge}(i)).
(2)~$\epsilon$-scale centered \emph{spherical} microtemplates realize the
local growth curves (Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge}(ii)); the corpus now lies on a
common sphere --- rescale to unit norm.
(3)~Choose $\epsilon$ small enough that the microstructure moves the global
spectrum by less than $\eta/2$.
(4)~Append the $\delta$-scale regular-simplex address block
(Lemmas~\ref{lem:preserve}--\ref{lem:address}), applicable verbatim to the
unit-normalized corpus.
(5)~Choose $\delta \ll \epsilon$ so its spectral and radial effects (the
deterministic Weyl cross-term bound $2\delta/\sqrt{n-1}$ included) are below
$\eta/2$.
(6)~Write $h$ as a convex combination of $k$-subset incidence vectors
(Corollary~\ref{cor:hypersimplex}).
(7)~Place the corresponding query mass on the simplex queries $q_S$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{What this proves.} There is no universal functional law
$G6 = f(G2, G3, G7, G8)$ over all finite retrieval experiments: G6 query
hubness is controlled by the query measure over top-$k$ capture cells; G3/G8
by the macro covariance; G2 by microstructure and radius distribution; and
base-to-base hub anatomy can remain \emph{exactly} fixed while query-to-base
hubness moves arbitrarily. This is stronger than the empirical observation
that our knobs happened to separate.

\paragraph{What it does not prove.} The construction is existential and
deliberately permissive: the address block may require $n-1$ extra dimensions;
the query and corpus marginals may differ substantially; independence is not
established under $\nuQ = \muC$, nor in fixed dimension; nothing guarantees
the constructed corpora resemble natural embeddings; and semantic, encoder,
normalization, and shared-support restrictions can reintroduce coupling. The
proper claim: \emph{there is no universal geometric necessity coupling these
quantities --- any coupling observed in a restricted generator or a real
embedding family arises from additional structural constraints.} That is
exactly what rounds 5--7 exhibit empirically.

\subsection{A generator-specific controllability test (registered)}
\label{sec:jacobian}

The universal theorem says nothing about whether independence holds
\emph{inside a realistic family}. For the round-7 generator define the
intervention map
$F : (\theta_s, \theta_q, \theta_r) \mapsto (G3, G6, G2)$, where $\theta_s$ =
\texttt{spectrum\_decay}, $\theta_q$ = \texttt{query\_tail}, and $\theta_r$ =
the local-scale-homogeneity control (\texttt{equalize}; in this family G2's
empirical controller is window homogenization rather than a radius spectrum,
a fidelity note the experiment records). If the Jacobian
\[
J = \begin{pmatrix}
\partial G3/\partial\theta_s & \partial G3/\partial\theta_q & \partial G3/\partial\theta_r\\
\partial G6/\partial\theta_s & \partial G6/\partial\theta_q & \partial G6/\partial\theta_r\\
\partial G2/\partial\theta_s & \partial G2/\partial\theta_q & \partial G2/\partial\theta_r
\end{pmatrix}
\]
has full rank at the round-7 solution, the inverse-function theorem gives
\emph{local independent controllability} within the actual family.
Pre-registered expectations: $\theta_s$ dominates the G3 column, $\theta_q$
the G6 column, $\theta_r$ the G2 column; $J$ nonsingular with acceptable
condition number, stable across fresh seeds.

\paragraph{Result (central differences $\times$ 3 seeds, at the round-7
anatomy-clean point).} Mean Jacobian, rows $(G3, G6, G2)$:
\[
\bar J = \begin{pmatrix}
-9.94 & -0.0067 & -0.0012\\
-0.074 & \phantom{-}0.048 & -0.0030\\
\phantom{-}0.44 & -0.011 & \phantom{-}0.0032
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad \det \bar J \neq 0,\ \ \kappa(\bar J) \approx 4.1\times10^{3}.
\]
The honest reading, expectation by expectation:
$\theta_s \to$ G3 is \emph{proven locally} ($-9.9$, two orders dominant in its
column) and was validated \emph{prospectively}: the follow-up round's
warm-start $\Delta\theta_s = +0.15$ moved G3 $2.50\times \to 1.83\times$
(linear extrapolation predicted $1.0\times$; half the linear step, as expected
on a curved response), and that warm-started point became the campaign's
solution. $\theta_q \to$ G6 is \emph{established large-signal but saturated
locally}: the per-instance derivative flips sign across seeds
($+0.19/{-0.12}/{+0.08}$, same-seed central differences --- landscape
heterogeneity, not estimator noise) because $\theta_q = 2.35$ sits near the
top of its $0.19\times\!\to\!2.20\times$ sweep. $\theta_r \to$ G2 is
\emph{locally inert} ($\sim\!10^{-3}$): with \texttt{size\_tail} $=0.16$ the
substrate is already homogeneous; G2's largest \emph{local} mover is in fact
$\theta_s$ ($+0.44$) via the colouring's effect on the $d_{10}$ window. The
registered condition-number expectation is \textbf{not met}: clean three-axis
local controllability is \emph{not} established at this operating point ---
one axis local, one large-signal, one exhausted --- and the
inverse-function-theorem claim is restricted accordingly. What the campaign
\emph{does} establish is compositional: large-signal interventions on the
three controls, applied in sequence across rounds 6--8, reached the joint
target.

\section{Experimental substrate and protocol}\label{sec:substrate}

The real target is a $1024$-dimensional Cohere Embed-V3 Wikipedia corpus with
its released held-out query set; fitting used $n = 8$k--$16$k working slices
of the non-sealed rows (a hash-designated 25\% of rows is sealed and was
never loaded in any experiment reported here). Geometry is measured on the
unit sphere by the benchmark's registered battery: exact blocked $k$-NN, one
neighbour structure per (corpus, $n$, subsample), gates read at
$k \in \{10, 30, 100\}$, in battery A (held-out corpus rows as queries) and
battery B (the real query set, or a generator's own registered query block).
Synthetic queries are held out from the \emph{same generated instance} as
their base --- an independently seeded draw is a different manifold
realization, and measuring against it silently penalizes every concentrated
family (the query-coupling artifact; \texttt{results/} in the repository).

The search harness wraps the benchmark's own battery in a shared
\texttt{evaluate\_fn} contract consumed by a 12-shard swarm random search
(480 evaluations per gating round, one CPU and 2\,GB per shard, on the NRP
Nautilus cluster) and by a structural-fuzzing adversary. Protocol: before
every gating round, a prediction and its falsifier were registered; screening
smokes, when they occurred, were disclosed in the registration; misses were
recorded as misses in the round documents that Table~\ref{tab:provenance}
summarizes.

\subsection{Separation III: query measure vs.\ local scale (round 7)}
\label{sec:sep3}
\begin{itemize}
\item The G2 mechanics: with per-query neighbour distances concentrated
  ($d_1 \approx d_{10}$, spread $\sim 10^{1/d_\ell}$), the mean ball-count
  curve is $\bar c(r) \approx 10\,F_{d_{10}}(r)$, so the fitted log--log slope
  over the estimator's window (the $[0.1,0.9]$ quantiles of $d_{10}$) is
  $\approx \log 9 / \log(\mathrm{window})$. Real's window is $1.17$
  $\Rightarrow$ slope $\approx 15$; the round-6 winner's window is $2.14$
  $\Rightarrow$ slope $\approx 2$. G2 is an across-query \emph{homogeneity}
  statistic, not a within-neighbourhood one.
\item Six corpus-side hub mechanisms fail on a metrically homogeneous substrate
  (scale equalization, global mean offset, Zipf duplicate families, per-point
  radius tilt, in-patch sub-clusters, cluster count) --- all reported as the
  negative results they are; a per-point radius law also leaves G2 unmoved,
  falsifying the naive ``radius spectrum'' account.
\item The diagnosis: real base$\to$base skew $\approx1.5$ vs.\ battery-B target
  $6.8$ --- the hubness lives in $\nuQ$. A same-instance Zipf query marginal

  \emph{improving} to $0.65\times$.

  produce query-induced G6 $\to$ search grows corpus-side central super-hubs
  $\to$ extreme density contrast $\to$ patch/plateau anatomy $\to$ G2
  collapses. The conflict was between \emph{the wrong mechanism for hubness}
  and realistic ball growth.
\item \textbf{Round-7 verdict --- the falsifier earns the paper its title.}

\section{Hubness is $H_k(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q},d)$: the capture-basin account}
\label{sec:hubness}

Let $\capture{x_j}$ denote the region of query space for which $x_j$ ranks in
the top $k$ under metric $d$ against corpus $\mathcal{C}$ --- the union of the
order-$k$ Voronoi cells whose retrieved set contains $j$
(\S\ref{sec:theory}). The observed reverse-neighbour count of $x_j$ under a
query workload $\mathcal{Q}$ is then, up to sampling noise,
\begin{equation}
N_k(x_j) \;\approx\; |\mathcal{Q}| \cdot \muQ\!\left(\capture{x_j}\right).
\label{eq:capture}
\end{equation}
Corpus geometry defines the basins; the query marginal decides how heavily
each is visited. The identical scalar skew can therefore arise from two
opposite worlds: a few \emph{huge basins} (corpus-side pathology --- extreme
central points whose basins swallow query space) or \emph{concentrated query
mass} falling on ordinary basins (the real system's mechanism,
\S\ref{sec:sep3}). Equation~\eqref{eq:capture} is why no corpus-only account
can close the gap: it is the coordinate form of the linear map of
Theorem~\ref{thm:polytope}, and its left factor is a property of
$\mathcal{Q}$, not of $\mathcal{C}$.

This account asks for a small but consequential correction in how hubness is
discussed. The classical results \cite{radovanovic10} establish hubness as a
phenomenon of dimensionality with a spatial-centrality account, measured
base$\to$base --- in the present notation $H_k(\mathcal{C},d)$, a property of
the corpus experiment with $\muQ = \muC$. That observable is real and
Table~\ref{tab:anatomy} reports it --- but it is a \emph{different
observable} from the query-measured $H_k(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q},d)$ that a
retrieval system experiences, and the two need not even be of the same order
(real corpus: base$\to$base skew $1.5$, query-measured skew $6.8$).
Hubness-reduction methods \cite{schnitzer12,zelnik04} act on the metric layer
(rescaling distances); \eqref{eq:capture} says they reshape basins, while
leaving untouched the possibility that the workload's illumination is itself
concentrated. Benchmark suites whose queries are drawn from the corpus
distribution \cite{aumuller20} structurally cannot exhibit the query-induced
regime --- and the effect can enter through subtle channels: in this
campaign's own registered harness, battery A drew its ``corpus-to-corpus''
queries as the \emph{first} held-out rows, and because Wikipedia rows arrive
topically ordered, those queries were topically clustered --- a de facto
concentrated query marginal hiding inside the corpus battery (base$\to$base
skew for uniformly drawn queries: $\approx 1.5$; for first-rows queries:
$9.4$ at $n=25$k). The protocol was re-registered with uniform sampling once
the decomposition made the asymmetry visible --- the query measure is not an
exotic addition to the experiment; it is present, somewhere, in every
retrieval measurement, and the only choice is whether it is controlled.

\section{The anatomy vector and the registered anatomy falsifier}\label{sec:anatomy}

Because a scalar underdetermines its mechanism, we propose reporting hubness
as a vector:
\[
A_{\mathrm{hub}} = (\text{q$\to$b skew},\ \text{b$\to$b skew},\ \max N_k,\
\mathrm{corr}(N_k, d_M),\ \mathrm{corr}(N_k, -d_{10}),\ \text{top-hub mass share}),
\]
where $d_M$ is Mahalanobis centrality and $d_{10}$ the tenth-neighbour
distance. The components are cheap (they reuse the reverse-$k$NN counts a
hubness measurement already computes) and they separate precisely the worlds
the scalar conflates: Table~\ref{tab:anatomy}'s two corpora agree in the
gate and disagree in every anatomy component.

The vector's operational form is the \emph{registered anatomy falsifier}. In
round 7 the registration required, beyond the six gate bands, that the
optimizer's best point keep base$\to$base skew $< 3$ --- the first
pre-registered check in the campaign that looks \emph{behind} a gate at the
structure producing it. It fired (\S\ref{sec:sep3}): the score-optimal point
satisfied all six gate bands on three fresh seeds while manufacturing corpus
super-hubs (skew $6.8$--$7.8$, max count ${\approx}350$), despite having a
genuine query-measure knob available. Without P2 that point would have
shipped as a success. The methodological consequence, adopted as the round-8
protocol and vindicated by its result, is that anatomy constraints belong
\emph{inside} the fitness, not only in post-hoc checks: an unpriced anatomy
is an invitation the optimizer accepts.

The same move generalizes gate by gate. G1's scalar (two-NN intrinsic
dimension) deserves an anatomy separating the short-range near-duplicate
ladder from manifold-bulk dimension --- the real corpus's reading is pinned
by the former, a fact invisible in the scalar and decisive at scale
(\S\ref{sec:limits}). G3's scalar (effective rank) deserves the spectrum
\emph{shape} versus its participation-ratio summary. In each case the scalar
is the trace of a mechanism, and optimizing the trace without registering
the mechanism invites exactly the Goodharting round 7 exhibited.

\section{Implications}\label{sec:implications}

\emph{For benchmark design.} A corpus-only generator cannot reproduce
query-induced statistics --- not as a matter of tuning but of type: the
observable is a functional of $\muQ$, which the generator does not model.
Synthetic workloads therefore need an explicit, registered query process,
sharing the corpus's structural realization, with its own marginal. This is
now the admission convention in the benchmark this campaign served.

\emph{For ANN evaluation.} Same-G6 corpora with opposite anatomy are natural
differential test vectors: run exact $k$NN against HNSW, DiskANN, FAISS, and
cuVS builds; fp32 against quantized operating points; cosine against
Euclidean; alternative graph build orders and shardings --- with oracles on
hub-rank divergence and on anti-hub-query recall collapse. Aggregate recall
hides exactly the tail the decomposition exposes: a system can score
identically on two corpora sharing every scalar gate and fail
catastrophically on the queries that land in one corpus's anti-hub region.
The companion paper (\emph{Fuzzing the Bond Metric}) develops the coverage
signature over layer-separated cells and the differential harness.

\emph{For representation learning.} Apparent statistic trade-offs in learned
embeddings deserve the same suspicion the generator walls received here. An
architecture, like a generator family, is a control structure binding
geometric layers to shared mechanisms (normalization couples radial to
angular structure; attention temperature couples spectrum to clustering); an
observed trade-off across training runs is evidence about that control
structure, and the constructive question --- what factored mechanism would
dissolve it? --- is as answerable there as it was here.

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

\emph{Hubness.} The phenomenon --- some points appearing unusually often in
$k$NN lists as dimension grows --- was established with a spatial-centrality
account by Radovanovi\'c et al.~\cite{radovanovic10}, following the
distance-concentration literature \cite{beyer99}. Hubness-reduction methods
--- local scaling \cite{zelnik04}, mutual proximity \cite{schnitzer12} ---
transform distances so that reverse-neighbour counts flatten. In the present
frame these are metric-layer interventions on basin shapes; the
decomposition adds that the observable itself is a functional of the query
measure \eqref{eq:capture}, so an untransformed corpus under a concentrated
workload and a pathological corpus under a uniform workload can present the
same scalar --- a distinction invisible to corpus-side treatment and, per
Theorem~\ref{thm:independence}, unresolvable from corpus-only observables.

\emph{Difficulty measures and workload shift in ANN.} Relative contrast
\cite{he12} and local intrinsic dimensionality \cite{levina04,facco17} are
corpus- or query-local difficulty accounts; recent graph-native measures
(Steiner-hardness \cite{steiner24}) explicitly tie difficulty to the index
structure, and out-of-distribution query work \cite{jaiswal22} documents that
query/corpus mismatch changes graph-ANN behaviour --- both consonant with
treating $(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{Q})$ jointly. Standard benchmark practice
\cite{aumuller20,simhadri21} fixes real query sets but characterizes
datasets by corpus statistics; the decomposition explains why that
characterization underdetermines system behaviour and what to report
instead.

\emph{Intrinsic-dimension estimation.} TwoNN \cite{facco17} and maximum-
likelihood \cite{levina04} estimators read short-range distance ratios; their
sensitivity to near-duplicates and sampling density is exactly the
local-scale layer's signature, and \S\ref{sec:limits} reports it as the
binding constraint at scale.

\emph{Fuzzing and property-based testing.} The registered-falsifier
discipline and the companion paper's coverage signature transfer
methodology from coverage-guided fuzzing \cite{klees18} and property-based
testing \cite{claessen00}: treat a corpus generator as a program under test,
geometric cells as coverage, and gate-satisfying-but-anatomy-violating
corpora as the crashing inputs.

\section{Limitations and the road to a theorem}\label{sec:limits}

\emph{Corroboration, not universal confirmation.} The empirical chain is
three separations on one real corpus family (text embeddings, one model
class) and one generator lineage. The theorems are existential: they forbid
universal coupling laws but do not predict which couplings a particular
family exhibits.

\emph{The scale postscript --- reported as the miss it is.} After the
campaign closed at its fitting scale ($n=8$k), the benchmark's registered
grid admission ($n$ from 25k to 200k, $\pm15\%$ equivalence bands, scaling-
exponent criteria) rejected the round-8 candidate: 0 of 24 cells. The
rejection map is itself decomposition-shaped, and each miss lands in a
nameable layer. The G3/G4 miss is flat in $n$ --- a mis-tuned colouring
(metric layer; the knob exists). G1 drifts upward with $n$ ($+0.04$--$0.06$
per decade) where the real corpus is $n$-flat --- real's two-NN reading is
pinned by a graded short-range ladder (nearest-neighbour distances at
\emph{fractional} scales: 1\%-quantile $0.375$ vs.\ median $0.86$) that
uniform-sparse patches lack; this is the G1 anatomy of \S\ref{sec:anatomy},
now load-bearing. And every scaling-exponent failure is G6 growth: a fixed
query concentration gives $+0.13$/decade where the real workload gives
$+0.22$ --- capture basins shrink as ${\sim}1/n$ while real's per-basin
query mass does not, so $\muQ$ is $n$-coupled. Both frontier mechanisms
(the graded ladder; the $n$-coupled illumination) entered the campaign as
predictions of the decomposition rather than post-hoc repairs; their
registered test is the current round.

\emph{What a theorem needs.} A formal generative class in which the four
layers are independently parameterized, with proofs that each gate is
invariant to the other layers' controls within stated ranges; candidate
assumptions: unit-sphere metric, sub-Gaussian patches, capture-basin
regularity, query support contained in the corpus's structural realization.

\emph{Couplings that are real, not representational.} The $s^{-d_\ell}$
amplification is a genuine cross-layer sensitivity, not an artifact;
normalization couples radial to angular structure; and encoder families may
impose joint constraints a factored generator does not. The decomposition
should be expected to fail exactly where such shared physical mechanisms
exist --- finding them is what the framework is \emph{for}: a coupling that
survives mechanism-splitting is a discovery about the system, not about the
statistics.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
Six rounds were spent solving a corpus-side problem whose target lived
substantially in the query marginal; when the right knob finally existed, the
optimizer preferred to rebuild the old pathology, and only a falsifier that
looked behind the scalar caught it; pricing that anatomy into the objective
and taking one derivative-informed step then reached, in a single round, the
joint target eight rounds of scalar-gate search could not --- real's numbers
by real's mechanism, stable across seeds. The statistics were never in
conflict; the mechanisms were. The registered scale grid then rejected the
same candidate beyond its fitting regime (\S\ref{sec:limits}) --- and each
miss landed in a nameable layer, with the two frontier mechanisms entering as
predictions of the decomposition rather than repairs. Embedding geometry
should be reported as an experiment $(X,\muC,\nuQ,d,k)$, its hubness as an
anatomy, and its trade-offs as claims about control structure --- claims that
a small amount of construction, or one pre-registered look behind a gate, can
refute.

and raw numbers: \url{https://github.com/ahb-sjsu/openvector-bench}
(\texttt{results/}, \texttt{spec/BOND\_METRIC.md}). Sealed evaluation rows were
never loaded during any experiment reported here.
